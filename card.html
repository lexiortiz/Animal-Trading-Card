Emperor Penguins

A colony of penguins swimming in the water is called a raft; on land, they're called a waddle!

  • Scientific Name: Aptenodytes forsteri
  • Average Length: 45"
  • Average Lifespan: 15 - 20 years
  • Diet: Carnivore

Emperor penguins are large flightless birds found south of the equator. They survive in Antarctica by being adaptable and swimming to feed their families. When a female penguin is courted and lays an egg, she immediately leaves the egg with a male penguin, who warms and protects the egg for 65 days until the female returns with food.
